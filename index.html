Frequently Asked Questions:

What should I eat before a workout?

A balanced pre-workout meal includes carbohydrates for energy and a small amount of protein to support muscle function. Options like a banana with peanut butter, Greek yogurt with fruit, or a small bowl of oatmeal work well. Try to eat 1-3 hours before exercising.

How often should I work out to see results?

The frequency depends on your fitness goals, but for general fitness, aim for at least 3-4 workouts per week. This can include a mix of strength training, cardio, and flexibility exercises. Consistency is key to seeing progress.

Can I lose weight by just dieting without exercising?

While diet plays a major role in weight loss, combining it with exercise can help you lose fat while preserving muscle. Exercise also improves overall health, boosts metabolism, and supports mental well-being.

How much protein do I need for muscle gain?

Protein needs vary based on activity level and body weight. For muscle gain, aim for about 1.6-2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily. Good sources include lean meats, eggs, dairy, beans, and legumes.

Is it okay to work out every day?

Yes, but it’s important to allow for recovery time, especially for high-intensity workouts. Alternating workout types and focusing on different muscle groups each day can help prevent overuse injuries and burnout.

Do I need supplements for muscle gain or weight loss?

Supplements can help fill nutritional gaps but are not essential for most people. Focus on a balanced diet first, and consider supplements like protein powder or vitamins only if recommended by a healthcare provider.
